I am finishing a bachelor's degree in Honours Math & CS at McGill University and Mila - Québec AI Institute, where I do research on reinforcement learning and optimization theory.

In Fall 2026, I will be starting a PhD in CS at the California Institute of Technology. I'm part of the Caltech AI & ML Group and the Chen Instiute for Social and Decision Neuroscience. I'm grateful to be supported by the Kortschak Graduate Fellowship in the CMS Department.

My current research interests span foundations of generative models, reinforcement learning, optimization dynamics, and applications to scientific discovery such as healthcare and cognitive neuroscience.

Specifically, I study reasoning and planning in agentic frameworks and foundation models, their representation geometry that makes downstream optimization well-conditioned, and generalizable discovery strategies under structured environments with verifiable feedback. Ultimately, I aim to develop AI systems that can interact and reason with limited supervision.

Feel free to reach out to me in case you have any questions or want to chat about research! I am always open to new collaborations.

In 2025, I led a small team of software developers at Hortus AI. We created a marketplace for public-sector AI that's designed to make partnerships transparent, ethical, and easy to navigate.
