I'm grateful to be supported by the Kortschak Graduate Fellowship in the CMS Department.

Research

My research primarily focuses on efficient reasoning in scalable settings that require deep exploration and robust generalization.

I am particularly interested in reinforcement learning and algorithmic decision-making as foundations for building adaptive, verifiable reasoning systems that can be trained and improved through post-training, test-time scaling, and self-improvement, and in how these ideas extend to generative and foundation models that can represent and interact with complex environments. I also study optimization and training dynamics that enable stable and efficient learning in such settings.

More broadly, I aim to understand how structure, feedback, and inductive biases can enable generalizable reasoning and discovery. I am particularly interested in building systems that can learn, explore, and reason in structured environments, with applications to mathematical and scientific discovery.

Feel free to reach out if you're interested in related problems or potential collaborations.
