Hi! I'm Alex, a final year Joint Honours Mathematics and Computer Science student at McGill University.

My research spans both machine learning and mathematics, with a focus on understanding and designing learning systems through theoretical and empirical lenses. In particular, I have worked on:

  • Reinforcement learning: Deep exploration and posterior sampling algorithms; Reinforcement learning from AI feedback and reward alignment.
  • Multimodal learning: Continual learning, reward alignment and reasoning in vision-language models.
  • Optimization theory: High-dimensional stochastic optimization and random matrix theory for analyzing training dynamics of deep models; Variational and nonsmooth analysis for studying Lipschitz stability and implicit network optimization.
  • Applied mathematics and statistics: Singularity formation in evolutionary PDEs using neural operators; Bayesian inference and active learning with connections to gaussian processes.

I have been very fortunate to be advised by Professors Doina Precup, Elliot Paquette, Siamak Ravanbakhsh, and Tim Hoheisel at McGill University, Mila - Québec AI Institute and Centre de recherches mathématiques. I have also had the privilege of collaborating closely with Professors Anima Anandkumar and Thomas Y. Hou's research groups at CMS, California Institute of Technology.
